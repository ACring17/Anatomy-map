Muscle Directory

Below is a list of all the major muscles. Find the group of muscles that interest you and explore. This guide will help you understand to how to train for a specific area on your body or what injuries to look out for.

Muscle Groups
Muscle Group Picture Main movement Exercises/Stretches
Chest
(pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, serratus anterior, subclavius)		 Pushing movement that brings the arms in medially. Stretches
  • Behind-the-back Elbow-to-elbow Grip
  • Above-the-head Chest Stretch
  • Bent-arm Wall Stretch
  • Extended Child’s Pose on Fingertips
  • Side Lying Parallel Arm Chest Stretch
Exercises
  • Barbell Bench Press
  • Cable Crossover
  • Dumbbell Incline Bench Press
  • Dumbbell Pullover
  • Push Up
Back
(trapezius, latissimus dorsi, levator scapulae, rhomboid major & minor)		 Back muscles work together to support the trunk and hold the body upright. They also allows the trunk to move, twist and bend in multiple directions. Stretches
  • Cat-Cow Stretch
  • Side-Lying Thoracic Rotation
  • Child’s Pose with Rotation
  • Thoracic Extension Over Foam Roller/Chair
  • Sphinx Pose
Exercises
  • Bent-Over Row
  • Shrug
  • Pull-Up
  • Seated Cable Row
  • One-Arm Row
Legs
(Anterior: quadriceps femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, sartorius, pectineus.
Posterior: biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus)		 Used to support the body, provide locomotion, and, in modified form, assist in capturing and eating food Stretches
  • Standing Quad Stretch
  • Hamstring/Calf Stretch
  • Inner Thigh Stretch
  • Supine Leg Stretch
Exercises
  • Squat
  • Deadlift
  • Dumbbell Lunge
  • Leg Press
  • Snatch
  • Power Clean
Arms
(biceps brachii, coracobrachialis, brachialis, triceps brachii)		 Used for grip and mobility of the upper limbs. Stretches
  • Standing Bicep Stretch
  • Seated Bicep Stretch
  • Wall Bicep Stretch
  • Behind-the-Head Triceps Stretch
  • Standing Bench Triceps Stretch
  • Towel triceps stretch
Exercises
  • Dumbbell Curl
  • Hammer Curl
  • Dumbbell Kick-Back
  • Overhead Triceps Extension
  • Skullcrusher
Abs
(external oblique, internal oblique, transversus abdominis, rectus abdominis, pyramidalis)		 Support the trunk, allow movement and hold organs in place by regulating internal abdominal pressure. The deep abdominal muscles, together with muscles in the back, make up your 'core' muscles and help keep your body stable and balanced, and protects your spine. Stretches
  • Cobra Pose
  • Cat-Cow stretch
  • Seated Side-Straddle stretch
  • Chest opener on an exercise ball
Exercises
  • Sit-Up
  • Leg Raise
  • Jackknife Sit-Up
  • Crunch
  • Reverse Crunch
Shoulders
(deltoid, teres major, supraspinatus, infraspinatus, subscapularis, teres minor)		 Stabilization of the shoulder joint and the elevation and rotation of the arm. Stretches
  • Across-the-chest stretch
  • Neck Release
  • Chest Expansion
  • Seated Twist
  • Shoulder Circles
  • Thread the Needle
Exercises
  • Lateral Raise
  • Front Raises
  • Bent-over Reverse Fly
  • Upright Row
  • Kettlebell Single-Arm Press
  • Half-kneeling Archer Row
